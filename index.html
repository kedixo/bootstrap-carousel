Storm Forms

Oak

When to use: Use Oak instead of Listy to produce shorter, chunky, sectioned articles that feature a mix of typographical styles, interplay between text and media, and plenty of vivid contextual media

For guidance on when and how to use Oak, contact News Design at #news-design, or the Digital Transition team at #scoophelp or dt@.

Full-Bleed Header

When to use: Full-bleed headers provide emphasis on the visual aspects of an article. Save full-bleed headers for articles with exceptional reporting, photography, or visuals that deserve extra attention from our readers.

For guidance on when and how to use full-bleed headers, contact the News Design team in Slack at #news-design

Chatblog

When to use: Use Chatblog for live news events that will unfold on a moment-by-moment basis — for example, a major sporting event — or for planned question-and-answer sessions, with or without reader-submitted comments and questions.

For guidance on when and how to use Chatblogs, contact the Digital Transition team at #newstools or dt@.

Live Briefing

When to use: Use Live Briefings to provide perspective on major news events that have a large scope, multiple storylines, and regular updates. Faster than a standard Scoop article, slower than Chatblog.

For guidance on when and how to use Live Briefings, contact the Digital Transition team at #scoophelp or dt@.

Photo Essay

When to use: Use photo essays to showcase our finest collections of visual media, with explainer text taking a backseat to a vivid narrative constructed mostly by our photo staff. Extra credit for an especially stunning lead photograph.

For guidance on when and how to use a photo essay, contact the Digital Transition team at #scoophelp or dt@.
