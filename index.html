Tools and Story Forms

Oak

a reimagined story creation tool, Oak is intuitive to use, and built to support an expressive range of storytelling.

When to use it: Use Oak when you have a variety of contextual media, complex typography needs, and a narrative that lends itself to an interplay between text and media.

How to get started: At this stage in the rollout, we're asking users to use Oak only for articles that would otherwise be Listys. Have a look at our Oak training and support website for information on how to get started.

Full-Bleed Header

Articles that place an iconic, gripping image at the top to set a mood, and draw extra attention to exceptional work.

When to use it: Full-bleed headers provide emphasis on the visual aspects of an article. Save full-bleed headers for articles with exceptional reporting, photography, or visuals that deserve extra attention from our readers.

How to get started: For guidance on using full-bleed headers, contact the News Design team at #news-design

Chatblog

An Interactive News tool that enables us to build intimate connections with readers by publishing live chats, with or without reader comments.

When to use it: Use Chatblog for live news events that will unfold on a moment-by-moment basis—for example, major sporting events—or for planned question-and-answer sessions, with or without reader-submitted comments.

How to get started: Have a look at our Chatblog training and support website for information on how to get started.

Live Briefing

Faster than a standard article, Live Briefings add style and structure to articles to provide readers with comprehensive digests of important news events.

When to use it: Use Live Briefings to provide perspective on major news events that have a large scope, multiple storylines, and regular updates. Faster than a standard Scoop article, slower than Chatblog.

How to get started: Have a look at our Live Briefing training and support website for information on how to get started.

Photo Essay

Photo essays showcase our photo desk's most exceptional work, using intermingled images and explainer text to provide a visual narrative with strong emotional impact.

When to use it: Use photo essays to showcase our finest collections of visual media, with explainer text taking a backseat to a vivid narrative constructed mostly by our photo staff. Extra credit for an especially stunning lead photograph.

How to get started: For guidance on using photo essays, contact the News Design team at #news-design

Documents

Document Cloud enables us to publish public documents with our articles—and it holds powerful reporting tools that can index hundreds of pages of information in seconds.

When to use it: Use Document Cloud in conjunction with Scoop Documents to publish any public document that relates directly to your reporting. Document Cloud also offers powerful reporting tools that can make your life better.

How to get started: Have a look at our Documents training and support website for information on how to get started.

Preview Maker

Preview Maker uses Google Documents—and the guidance of a news graphics editor—to power sophisticated interactive journalism with custom designs.

When to use it: Preview Maker requires dedicated assistance from our News Graphics and News Design teams—it should only be used for notable articles that deserve extra attention from our readers.

How to get started: For guidance on using Preview Maker, contact the News Graphics team at #graphics
